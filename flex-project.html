CIBC FLEX COMPETITION

jan - apr 2019

CIBC Digital hosts a design competition for their interns that runs throughout the term. We were split into four teams of six and each given a unique business problem. Our team was also assigned a sponsor and mentor. We had the whole term to come up with the best and most feasible solution and at the end of term, pitch our idea to a panel of four Digital VPs.

This is our design solution, At a Glance, which won first place.

OUR PROCESS

THE TEAM

Adeline (Digital Sales and Strategy), Anthony (Strategy), Daniel (Digital Sales and Strategy), Vidya (Agile Marketing)

I was the only designer on our team and was responsible for leading the design of our product.

PROBLEM

How do we build the bank of the future, inclusive of products and services, based on the needs and inputs of our clients?

This was the problem presented to us by our sponsor, the Senior Director and Head of Strategy at Simplii Financial. After asking him to elaborate some more on the issue, we were able to understand what he meant by such a broad topic.

“[Paraphrased] Currently, CIBC does not have a reliable way to collect feedback from its clients. However, feedback is essential to ensure that CIBC is delivering products and services that its clients actually use. We do not want to be wasting resources on products that clients find unnecessary. We instead want to know how to improve our existing services or find opportunities for new ones.”

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

We started off by researching the different ways that companies collect feedback. What we found was that most feedback collection methods are generic and unappealing. It was therefore no surprise to us when we learned that only 1 in 4 people respond to online surveys.

RESEARCH

We wanted to determine why so few people respond to online surveys in the hopes of determining a set of criteria for our solution. Below are a few reasons that stood out to us the most.

It was essential for our feedback solution to be quick, engaging, and benefit the user in some way. It seemed that people were more willing to provide their personal information if they are given something of value in return.

IDEATION

The team sat together to brainstorm possible solutions.

Based on our research, our team noticed a trend towards companies providing personalized options for their customers.

This was what our mentor said about personalization in banking:

“[Paraphrased] A lot of banks have personalized data but it is difficult to know how to use this type of information. This is why tackling financial literacy can be such a sensitive topic. We want to help our clients, but we want them to still feel like they are making their own decisions. It is not up to the bank to judge our client’s spending, but perhaps guide them towards making a better choice.”

Below is a persona we used to help us in our ideation phase. It is based on our research as well as CIBC’s existing client personas.

SOLUTION

In our research of companies who are using personalization to engage with their customers, Spotify stood out to us the most with their Spotify Wrapped.

Our team wanted to introduce this idea to CIBC, by presenting clients with their financial information in a visual, easy to understand, and colourful format.

The goal of At a Glance would be to provide clients with engaging information that gives them autonomy to choose what they want to see but ultimately releases the burden of making complex financial decisions.

In return, clients will answer a quick survey that CIBC can use to collect feedback.

Overall, the solution fits our criteria of being quick, engaging, and beneficial to the client.

REFLECTION

Participating in the FLEX Competition allowed me to gain a lot of insight into how to work with a team of diverse backgrounds. Each and every member of our team contributed their skillset to the problem.

I was also able to get a lot of experience working with a UI kit in Sketch, as well as practice visual design. Overall, this was my first full-scale product design experience and was happy that CIBC gave us this opportunity.
