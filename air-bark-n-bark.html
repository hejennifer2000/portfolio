AIR BARK N BARK

February 2019 - PRESENT

BACKGROUND

This was a project that my teammate and I worked on during a two-day Hackathon in February, 2019. It is inspired by our classmate, who told us she wished there existed a platform through which she could “rent a dog”.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Below are some dog-sitting resources currently available through web and mobile. I studied these to learn about the process of finding a pet-sitter, including what owners are looking for in a reliable sitter, how to filter search results, and the overall flow of the process.

OPPORTUNITY

Currently, there are many resources that help owners connect with experienced dog-sitters that expect pay. However, is there also an opportunity to help ordinary dog-lovers connect with the dogs in their area without the cost of ownership?

How do we help dog-lovers connect and engage with the dogs in their area?

PERSONA

The persona below is created based on comments made by our classmate to help understand why someone might need this type of product.

Based on our persona, it is clear that Elizabeth is a dedicated student with an unpredictable schedule. As a student, her stress levels vary depending on her workload. Therefore, it is important that she is easily able to access the resources that she needs as soon as possible.

OTHER CONSIDERATIONS

1. Most users are not experienced dog-sitters.

How will owners trust that the users taking care of their dogs are reliable and not malicious?

2. Some users will also be owners that would like to rent other dogs.

How do we make the app flexible enough to allow for communication both ways?

IDEATION AND SKETCHES

Through our ideation process, we identified five main points of interaction to consider in our design. These were implemented onto our navigation bar.

1. Onboarding and Profile

This is the stage where a user opens their Air Bark n Bark account and begins to set up their profile.

2. Explore

In the explore stage, users will search for and browse the dogs available in their area.

3. Saved

In this stage, the user has easy and convenient access to the dogs they are interested in.

4. Messages

In this stage, the user has decided on which dogs they would like to rent and begins communication with the owner.

5. Bookings

Once they have confirmed a dog with its owner, the user can check their calendar to manage their bookings.

DESIGN

Because we were planning to design as many frames as possible within the alloted time, we created a design library from which we could easily grab the necessary components. Repeated components such as icons, buttons, and cards were used in our library.

We chose a moderate blue to use as our primary colour and paired it with a pale cyan. We also identified an additional red accent colour to contrast the blue.

We chose Questrial as our font for its clarity and playfulness. Similarly, the card components have a curved radius in order to create a more inviting look, keeping with the tone of our app.

Onboarding

Our persona indicates that our target audience does not have a lot of free time. Onboarding ensures that the user inputs the majority of their information at the beginning of their journey to optimize their experience later on. The goal of onboarding is to increase the user's chance of finding a trusting owner.

Profile

The profile allows owners to determine whether the user is a reliable candidate to take care of their dog.

Explore

Filtering search results gives control to the user, allowing them to find a dog that fits their location and schedule. Saving creates a shortlist of dogs that are now easily accessible to the user.

Messages

A user could be both an owner and a renter at the same time. Therefore, messages are divided into two tabs. The user can easily switch back and forth between tabs depending on what role they are currently taking on.

There also needed to be a way to differentiate between pending requests and messages that are already ongoing. Because requests are given greater importance, they are placed at the top in a card component.

Booking

Similarly, there needed to be a way for users who are both renters and owners to navigate between two schedules: meetings with others' dogs and meetings with their own dogs.

REFLECTION

Although I am uncertain about the practical feasibility of an app like Air Bark n Bark, working on this project gave me experience and insight into creating a new application. I now recognize the amount of work and critical thinking that goes into this process. There are many different complex points of interaction to consider and frames can connect in a multitude of different ways.

If this app is not practically feasible, I believe that it also provides the basic framework for many other booking apps (ex. finding a rental home, booking a hotel).

Similarly, I learned how to work with a teammate, allowing us to bounce ideas off one another. It helped to have another person to talk to when I was unsure how to present a certain piece of information or where to place a certain button.

I also learned how to start constructing a design library and when it may be beneficial to use one.
